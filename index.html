Western Australia R Group (WARG) meetups

In addition to the official pages of the meetup, please find below a brief summary of the previous meetings, with links to the presentations if available. There is also RNEWS page for R news.

  • errorWARG #5 22 Oct 2015

    Presentation by Juan Luong

    Juan's talk was on building web applications with Shiny, one of the prime RStudio projects. He reviewed the basic framework, motivating examples, key add-on's, useful links and other relevances.

    Presentation by W. Volk-Makarewicz

    Warren's talk was on the nonlinear machine learning dimensionality reduction technique known as t-distributed stochastic neighbor embedding, or t-SNE for short. This relatively new algorithm can be used for embedding high-dimensional data into a space of two or three dimensions, meaning that a usual scatterplot can be used to visualize a high-deminesional dataset.

    R News & Tips by Andrey Kostenko

    The highlights are: ● data.table 1.9.6 is now on CRAN ● R Newsfeeds and Cheatsheets ● Popular R time series book is now free ● R and MS PowerPoint (embed a Shiny App in your slides) ● Index of Online R Courses, October 2015

  • error_outlineWARG #4 10 Sep 2015

    Presentation by Tommy O'Dell

    Tommy focused on R package dplyr . Using a well-known dataset, he showed how the six most useful functions of the package can be used for data manipulation. He then reviewed the use of pipe operators, a recent addition to R's best practices. The remainder of the talk was devoted to Tommy's own R package rdplyr, which looks and smells much like dplyr. The slides and some additional materials are also available from GitHub here and here.

    R News & Tips by Andrey Kostenko

    The highlights are: ● 125 new R packages a month - how to keep up? Cranberries, MRAN, CRAN, METACRAN, Crantastic ● the man who revolutionised R, hadleyverse (30 slides from Barry Rowlingson exemplifying the use of Hadley's packages, including dplyr), interviews with Hadley Wikcham ● two new MOOCs on R from Microsoft (starting after September 20) ● R at Microsoft (45 slides from Revolution Analytics)

  • error_outlineWARG #2 25 Jun 2015

    Presentation by Andrey Kostenko

    Andrey talked on four more prominent frameworks / technologies for interactive data visualization that are available for R users: ● htmlwidgets framework that bridges R and JavaScript world, ● rCharts package that combines many JavaScript data visualisation libraries, ● Google Chart API, and ● Plotly API. Upon briefly reviewing and exemplifying these alternatives, he concluded that all were good but the most recently proposed htmlwidgets framework seemed best thanks to its flexibility, generality and direct support from RStudio developers.

  • infoWARG #1 28 May 2015

    Presentation by Alain Lesaffre

    Alain shared his tips and advice on using R packages caret and mboost useful to those interested in data science in general and machine learning in particular. Through example he reviewed the caret's main function train() and three groups of parameters associated with it. He concluded that the package helps tune parameters of individual models and select models that predict better from over 140 alternatives.
